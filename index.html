The Academic Incentive Ontology

About

The Academic Incentive Ontology (AIO) is an OWL ontology which captures the difficult semantics of Blockchain-based data sharing. The AIO attempts to handle data-sharing while utilizing blockchain to implement an incentivize scheme to encourage peer-to-peer data sharing. The ontology has been developed as part of the ongoing SCIENCE project within Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute's Tetherless World Constellation and Institute for Data Exploration and Analysis.

Abstract

In many fields, data collection is slow and rigorous, meaning conclusive findings may take years to produce. This can lead to the reproduction of similar datasets, while leaving datasets with multiple implications abeyant. Further, there is a lack of complete and useful open datasets in many different fields, which leaves researchers with no choice but to collect their own data. There is a rising need for a secure data sharing ecosystem that recommends, incentivizes, and rewards collaborative efforts between researchers and research groups. Permissionless, public blockchains provide the necessary framework for fair data sharing between parties who have not established a formal trust relationship. We designed the Academic Incentive Ontology built to capture the activities associated with blockchain-based research data sharing, as well as a corresponding incentive scheme, which includes a reputation-based peer review system and a generalized metric to provide recommendations and ratings of possible collaborators. We present a use case via a decentralized application powered by smart contracts, which implements our proposed blockchain based-solution, and we provide a set of competency questions with accompanying SPARQL queries that are used to evaluate the ontologyâ€™s coverage of the application and its domain.
