Ontology Specification Draft

Abstract

The Data Sharing Ontology (DSO) intends to provide a semantic model to power a blockchain application to handle, confirm, and incentivize research data sharing. The ontology is built upon basic blockchain components such as addresses and transaction hashes and uses simple logic such that it could be easily handled by smart contracts

Introduction back to ToC

With data becoming so important, not just in academia, there has been a push to utilize new technology to maximize dataâ€™s potential and economic ability. Within academia, this is contradicted by the competitiveness of the academic research space. Although academic research is deeply co-operative, researchers also build ideas on top of their rival's as they compete for citations, acceptance, and recognition. This is coupled with a lacking of technological advancements for handling data sharing. Handling data requires accurate metadata such that it is usable even in the passing of hands. We present the Data Sharing Ontology (DSO) which describes a blockchain-based protocol for data sharing between two researchers. We argue that the nature of blockchain is the perfect environment for trustless and incentivized data sharing. The ontology includes a two-part incentive mechanism designed to confirm citations and reward reproducible research methods.
