Abstract

In many fields, data collection is slow and rigorous, meaning conclusive findings may take years to produce. This can lead to the reproduction of similar datasets, while leaving datasets with multiple implications abeyant. Further, there is a lack of complete and useful open datasets in many different fields, which leaves researchers with no choice but to collect their own data. There is a rising need for a secure data sharing ecosystem that recommends, incentivizes, and rewards collaborative efforts between researchers and research groups. Permissionless, public blockchains provide the necessary framework for fair data sharing between parties who have not established a formal trust relationship. We designed the Academic Incentive Ontology (AIO) to capture the activities associated with blockchain-based research data sharing and build a knowledge graph capturing the collaborations between researchers, as well as a corresponding incentive scheme to reward these collaborations. Our incentives also aim to consume large amounts of academic researcher data (i.e. h-index) from various web sources in a decentralized manner to output a useful metric by which peers can evaluate possible collaborators. We present a use case via a smart contract-powered application implementing the AIO's semantics and evaluate it's coverage with competency questions via Web3-based queries. Our work ultimately aims to mitigate data reproduction and create a more efficient research process by opening the doors to trustless, incentivized data sharing.

Introduction back to ToC

The Academic Incentive Ontology (AIO) intends to provide a semantic model to power a blockchain application to handle, confirm, and incentivize research data sharing. The ontology is built upon basic blockchain components such as addresses and transaction hashes and uses simple logic such that it could be easily handled by smart contracts.
